Research Poster Presentations

Psychology research on loneliness and adjustment

Project Type

Individually-led, team-supported, and faculty-directed

Duration

Winter 2019 -- present (multiple projects)

Role

Literature review, data analysis, poster configuration

Skills

Literature review, SPSS, academic writing

Challenge

Preparing posters to present research at regional, national and international conferences

These tasks are rarely accessible to undergraduate research assistants but are made possible in the Changz lab directed by Dr. Edward C. Chang.

Analysis

Statistical analysis with SPSS

We work with already collected datasets to identify interesting trends. Some frequently run analyses include hierarchical regression models, mediation models, and bootstrapping.

Literature review

Locating the context

I go through peer-reviewed journal databases (e.g., PsychInfo) to look for the importance of the topic, methodologies and findings in similar research, and what remains unknown. This process allows me to form a narrative of why our research is done.

Write-up and poster configuration

Edit, edit, and edit

During each project, lab members provide feedback on the poster drafts every day. I go through a large volume of iterations and stay open to feedback and different writing styles. Collaboratively, we push the poster to its best version.

By experimenting with new poster templates, we are also learning how to communicate our findings most effectively.

Presentation

Intellectual exchange with peer researchers

Research conferences offer great inspirations about unimagined research implications and future directions.

When presenting at the 2019 Michigan Undergraduate Psychology Research Conference, multiple researchers talked to me about how social networking sites could increase loneliness and therefore exacerbate the correlation found in our study. It was eye-opending!
